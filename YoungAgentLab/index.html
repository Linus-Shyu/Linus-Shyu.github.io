Overview ‹ Young Agent Lab

Advancing human capability by designing agent systems that learn, plan, and collaborate in the real world

A young-researcher collective bridging top-tier ML research with products, open source, and hackathon engineering.

Overview

Young Agent Lab is a collaborative research community — not a single institution, but a shared identity for young builders who pursue both rigorous research and real-world shipping.

The lab brings together agent systems, generative AI, and human-centered products — from efficient multimodal models to tool-using agents that deploy under real constraints.

Our motivation is to keep intelligent systems practical: fast enough to deploy, reliable enough to trust, and structured enough to improve over time. Members study dynamic compute allocation, agentic omni models, emotion-aware applications, and competitive engineering.

Attribution: Conference papers listed here are co-authored through member affiliations (e.g. Sphere Lab). Products, open-source tools, and hackathon projects are lab-adjacent work led by individual members and showcased as collective output.

Research Track

Top-tier publications in efficient video/flow generation and agentic omni models — advanced through collaborations with established research groups.

Builder Track

Shipped apps, open-source infrastructure, and hackathon prototypes — where ideas meet deadlines, users, and production constraints.
